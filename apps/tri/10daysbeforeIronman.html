10-Day Routine Before Ironman Full Distance

General Guidelines

  • Tapering: The focus is on reducing the volume of your workouts while maintaining intensity to keep your body primed.
  • Nutrition: Maintain a balanced diet with an emphasis on carbohydrates for energy storage.
  • Hydration: Increase water intake and consider electrolytes to ensure you're well-hydrated.
  • Sleep: Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep each night to allow for recovery.

Day 10

  • Morning: 45-minute swim at race pace (focus on form, technique, and pacing).
  • Afternoon: 60-minute easy bike ride (low intensity, keep cadence high).
  • Evening: 15-minute core workout and stretching.

Day 9

  • Morning: 30-minute run at race pace (focus on maintaining form and breathing).
  • Afternoon: 1-hour easy bike ride (low intensity, focus on smooth pedaling).
  • Evening: Yoga or light stretching session (focus on flexibility and relaxation).

Day 8

  • Morning: 1-hour swim with intervals (include 4-5 short sprints to keep intensity up).
  • Afternoon: Rest or optional 30-minute easy bike ride (just to keep legs loose).
  • Evening: Mental rehearsal (visualize the race, focus on transitions, and think through your race day plan).

Day 7

  • Morning: 45-minute run at moderate pace (include 2-3 short sprints).
  • Afternoon: 1-hour easy bike ride (focus on smooth transitions between pace changes).
  • Evening: Massage or foam rolling session to alleviate muscle tension.

Day 6

  • Morning: 1-hour swim (race pace, focus on open-water skills if possible).
  • Afternoon: Rest or optional 30-minute easy bike ride (focus on relaxation).
  • Evening: Short stretching session and early to bed.

Day 5

  • Morning: 30-minute easy run (race pace, include a few short bursts).
  • Afternoon: 45-minute bike ride with a few race-pace efforts.
  • Evening: Review race strategy, nutrition plan, and double-check all gear.

Day 4

  • Morning: 30-minute swim (focus on form, technique, and breathing).
  • Afternoon: Rest or 20-minute easy bike ride (focus on relaxation).
  • Evening: Light stretching or yoga and visualization exercises.

Day 3

  • Morning: 20-minute run with a few short sprints (race pace, focus on relaxation and enjoyment).
  • Afternoon: 30-minute bike ride (easy, just to keep the legs fresh).
  • Evening: Massage or foam rolling session. Early to bed.

Day 2

  • Morning: 30-minute swim (easy, focus on form and breathing).
  • Afternoon: Rest or short 20-minute easy bike ride (just to keep the body moving).
  • Evening: Prepare all race day gear, review race day logistics, and ensure hydration.

Day 1 (Day Before Race)

  • Morning: 15-minute easy swim (keep it very relaxed, focus on feeling good in the water).
  • Afternoon: Light stretching, check-in to race venue, and prepare transition bags.
  • Evening: Eat a carbohydrate-rich dinner, relax, and go to bed early.

Race Day (Day 0)

  • Morning: Wake up early, eat a light, familiar breakfast.
  • Pre-Race: Arrive early, set up transition, and do a short warm-up (light jogging, swimming, or dynamic stretching).
  • Race: Stay calm, follow your race plan, and enjoy the experience!

Final Tips

  • Nutrition: Carbo-load in the final 2-3 days to ensure glycogen stores are topped off.
  • Mental Preparation: Spend time each evening focusing on positive thoughts, visualizing the race, and calming any nerves.
  • Rest: Reduce volume significantly in the final three days to allow your body to fully recover and be race-ready.
